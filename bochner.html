1 Introduction

Notes on the Bochner formula. Section 3 of (Li 2012) provides a more general account of the Bochner formula for differential forms, while a few exercises and facts found in (Chow, Lu, and Ni 2006) provide a shorter account for the Bochner formula for functions.

2 The Bochner Formula

Let \((M,g)\) be a Riemannian manifold. Recall that for a function \(f\), the Hessian in local coordinates is given by

\[ \nabla_i\nabla_j f := (\nabla\nabla f)\left(\partial_i, \partial_j\right) = \frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial_i\partial_j} - \Gamma^k_{ij}\partial_k f. \]

Then we define the Laplacian (also called the Laplace-Beltrami operator) as the divergence of the gradient

\[ \Delta = \text{div}\nabla = \text{trace}_g\nabla^2 = g^{ij}\nabla_i\nabla_j =: \nabla_i\nabla_i, \]

where for the last “equality” it is understood that we are summing over repeated indices.

Also recall the commutation formulas (Ricci identities) for a 1-form \(\alpha\), then

\[ \left(\nabla_i\nabla_j - \nabla_j\nabla_i\right)\alpha_k = -{R_{ijk}}^\ell \alpha_{\ell}. \]

Then note that we have

\[\begin{aligned} \Delta\nabla_i f &= \nabla_j\nabla_j\nabla_i f \\ &= \nabla_j\nabla_i\nabla_j f \\ &= \nabla_i\nabla_j\nabla_j f - {R_{jij}}^\ell \nabla_j f \end{aligned}\]

where we have used the symmetricity of the Hessian in the second equality. Then tracing over indices, we have

\[ \Delta\nabla_i f = \nabla_i \Delta f + R_{ij}\nabla_j f. \]

Now we are in a position to prove the Bochner formula for \(f\)

\[\begin{aligned} \Delta|\nabla f|^2 &= \nabla_i\nabla_i|\nabla_j f|^2 \\ &= 2\nabla_i(\nabla_i\nabla_j f \nabla_j f) \\ &= 2\nabla_i\nabla_j f \nabla_i\nabla_j f + 2 \Delta \nabla_j f \nabla_j f \\ &= 2 |\nabla\nabla f|^2 + 2\Delta \nabla_j f \nabla_j f \\ &= 2 |\nabla\nabla f|^2 + 2(\nabla_j \Delta f + R_{ji}\nabla_i f)\nabla_j f, \end{aligned}\]

and interchanging \(i,j\), we obtain

\[ \Delta|\nabla f|^2 = 2|\nabla\nabla f|^2 + 2 \nabla_i f \nabla_i \Delta f + 2 R_{ij}\nabla_i f \nabla_j f. \]
