Differential Manifolds

We begin with some basic definitions.

Definition: A differential manifold of dimension n is a set M and a family of injective mappings xα : Uα ⊂ ℝn → M of open sets Uα of ℝn into M such that

  1. ⋃αxα(Uα) = M,
  2. for any pair α, β with xα(Uα) ∩ xβ(Uβ) = W ≠ 0, the sets xα − 1(W) and xβ − 1(W) are open sets in ℝn and the mappings xβ − 1 ∘ xα are differentiable,
  3. the family {(Uα,xα)} is maximal relative to the first two conditions.

The pair (Uα,xα) is called a parametrizations or system of coordinates of M at p. The sets xα(Uα) are called a coordinate neighborhood at p. A family {(Uα,xα)} satisfying conditions (1) and (2) above is called a differential structure on M.
