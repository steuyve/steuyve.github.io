2 Initial Setup

Let \(p > 1\). A function \(v: M \to \mathbb{R}\) is called a \(p\)-harmonic function if it satisfies

\[ \text{div}\left(\left|\nabla v\right|^{p-2}\nabla v\right) = 0. \](2.1)

Note that when \(p = 2\), this reduces to the ordinary Laplace equation. Letting \(u := -(p-1)\log{v}\), then (2.1) becomes

\[ \text{div}\left(\left|\nabla u\right|^{p-2}\nabla u\right) - \left|\nabla u\right|^p = 0. \](2.2)

Note that when \(p = 1\), (2.2) above is the equation to the level-set formulation of the inverse mean curvature flow on a co-compact subdomain \(\Omega \subset \mathbb{R}^n\).

Let \(f := \left|\nabla u\right|^2\). Then the LHS of (2.2) becomes

\[ \begin{aligned} \text{div}\left(\left|\nabla u\right|^{p-2}\nabla u\right) &= \text{div}\left(f^{p/2-1}\nabla u\right) \\ &= f^{p/2-1}\text{div}\left(\nabla u\right) + \left\langle \nabla u, \nabla\left(f^{p/2-1}\right)\right\rangle \\ &= f^{p/2-1}\Delta u + \left(\frac{p}{2}-1\right)f^{p/2-2}\left\langle\nabla f, \nabla u\right\rangle. \end{aligned} \]

and the RHS of the same equation becomes \(\left|\nabla u\right|^{p} = \left(\left|\nabla u \right|^2\right)^{p/2} = f^{p/2}\). So we have that for \(f\), (2.2) is equivalent to

\[ f^{p/2-1}\Delta u + \left(\frac{p}{2}-1\right)f^{p/2-2}\langle\nabla f, \nabla u\rangle = f^{p/2}. \](2.3)

3 A Nonlinear Bochner-type Formula

Assume \(u, f > 0\) and \(u\) is a solution to (2.2) above. Let us denote by \(\Lambda_p\) the operator on the LHS of (2.2), that is,

\[ \Lambda_p(\varphi) := \text{div}\left(\left|\nabla \varphi\right|^{p-2}\nabla \varphi\right) - \left|\nabla \varphi\right|^p \]

and let \(\mathcal{L}\) denote the linearization of \(\Lambda_p\) at \(u\).

We can compute what \(\mathcal{L}\) must be by computing \(\frac{d}{d\varepsilon}\Big|_{\varepsilon=0}\Lambda_p(u+\varepsilon\varphi)\), i.e., taking the derivative at 0 of a small perturbation of \(\Lambda_p(u)\). Note that we have \(\nabla(u+\varepsilon\varphi) = \nabla u + \varepsilon\nabla\varphi\) and that

\[ \left|\nabla(u+\varepsilon\varphi)\right|^p = \left(\langle\nabla u + \varepsilon\nabla\varphi, \nabla u + \varepsilon\nabla\varphi\rangle\right)^{p/2} = \left(|\nabla u|^2 + 2\varepsilon\langle\nabla u,\nabla \varphi\rangle + \varepsilon^2|\nabla\varphi|^2\right)^{p/2} \]

So we have that

\[ \begin{aligned} \frac{d}{d\varepsilon}\Big|_{\varepsilon=0}|\nabla(u+\varepsilon\varphi)|^p &= \frac{d}{d\varepsilon}\Big|_{\varepsilon=0}\left(|\nabla u|^2 + 2\varepsilon\langle\nabla u,\nabla \varphi\rangle + \varepsilon^2|\nabla\varphi|^2\right)^{p/2} \\ &= \frac{p}{2}\left(|\nabla u|^2 + 2\varepsilon\langle\nabla u, \nabla \varphi\rangle + \varepsilon^2|\nabla\varphi|^2\right)^{p/2-1}\left(2\langle\nabla u, \nabla\varphi\rangle + 2\varepsilon|\nabla\varphi|^2\right)\Big|_{\varepsilon=0} \\ &= p\left(|\nabla u|^2\right)^{p/2-1}\langle\nabla u,\nabla\varphi\rangle \\ &= pf^{p/2-1}\langle\nabla u,\nabla\varphi\rangle. \end{aligned} \](3.1)

Similarly, we can evaluate \(\frac{d}{d\varepsilon}\Big|_{\varepsilon=0}\) of the expression inside the \(\text{div}\) operator. We have

\[ \begin{aligned} \frac{d}{d\varepsilon}\Big|_{\varepsilon=0}\left|\nabla(u+\varepsilon\varphi)\right|^{p-2}\nabla(u+\varepsilon\varphi) &= \frac{d}{d\varepsilon}\Big|_{\varepsilon=0}\left(|\nabla u|^2 + 2\varepsilon\langle\nabla u,\nabla \varphi\rangle + \varepsilon^2|\nabla\varphi|^2\right)^{p/2-1}\nabla(u+\varepsilon\varphi) \\ &\qquad + \left(|\nabla u|^2 + 2\varepsilon\langle\nabla u,\nabla \varphi\rangle + \varepsilon^2|\nabla\varphi|^2\right)^{p/2-1}\frac{d}{d\varepsilon}\Big|_{\varepsilon=0}(\nabla u + \varepsilon\nabla\varphi) \\ &= \left(\frac{p}{2}-1\right)\left(|\nabla u|^2\right)^{p/2-1}2\langle\nabla u, \nabla \varphi\rangle\nabla u + \left(|\nabla u|^2\right)^{p/2-1}\nabla\varphi \\ &= (p-2)f^{p/2-2}\langle\nabla u,\nabla\varphi\rangle\nabla u + f^{p/2-1}\nabla\varphi. \\ \end{aligned} \](3.2)

Notice that the tensor \(\nabla u \otimes \nabla u\) applied to the vector \(\nabla \varphi\) in coordinates is given by

\[ \begin{aligned} (\nabla u \otimes \nabla u)(\nabla\varphi) &= \left(u_i u_j dx^i\otimes dx^j\right)\left(g^{k\ell}\varphi_k\partial_\ell\right) \\ &= u_iu_jdx^i\left(g^{k\ell}\varphi_k\partial_\ell\right)dx^j \\ &= u_iu_jg^{k\ell}\varphi_k\delta^i_\ell dx^j \\ &= u_iu_j g^{ki}\varphi_kdx^j = \langle\nabla u,\nabla\varphi\rangle \nabla u. \end{aligned} \]

In view of this, we can define the tensor \(A:= \text{id} + (p-2)\frac{\nabla u \otimes \nabla u}{f}\) and rewrite the last line of (3.2) as

\[ \frac{d}{d\varepsilon}\Big|_{\varepsilon=0}\left|\nabla(u+\varepsilon\varphi)\right|^{p-2}\nabla(u+\varepsilon\varphi) = f^{p/2-1}A(\nabla\varphi). \]

So commuting \(\frac{d}{d\varepsilon}\) past the \(\text{div}\) operator, and combining the above with the last line of (3.1), all together we have

\[ \mathcal{L}(\varphi) = \frac{d}{d\varepsilon}\Big|_{\varepsilon=0}\Lambda_p(u + \varepsilon\varphi) = \text{div}\left(f^{p/2-1}A(\nabla\varphi)\right) - pf^{p/2-1}\langle\nabla u,\nabla\varphi\rangle. \](3.3)

With this explicit expression for \(\mathcal{L}\), Kotschwar and Ni then show the following lemma.

Lemma 3.1 ((Kotschwar and Ni 2009), Lemma 2.1). With \(u, f, A, \mathcal{L}\) as above, we have the following expression for \(\mathcal{L}(f)\).

\[ \mathcal{L}(f) = 2f^{p/2-1}\left(u_{ij}^2 + R_{ij}u_iu_j\right) + \left(\frac{p}{2}-1\right)|\nabla f|^2f^{p/2-2}, \]

where \(u_{ij}\) is the Hessian of \(u\), \(R_{ij}\) is the Ricci curvature of \(M\).

Proof. The expression comes from computing \(\mathcal{L}(f)\) using the expression we got in (3.3) above, and combining with the Bochner formula for \(f\).

Recall that the Bochner formula is given by the following.

\[ \Delta f = 2u_{ij}^2 + 2\langle\nabla\Delta u, \nabla u\rangle + 2R_{ij}u_iu_j. \](3.4)

By the definition of \(A\) and evaluating, we have

\[ \begin{aligned} \text{div}\left(f^{p/2-1}A(\nabla f)\right) &= \text{div}\left(f^{p/2-1}\left(\nabla f + (p-2)\frac{\nabla u \otimes \nabla u}{f}\nabla f\right)\right) \\ &= \text{div}\left(f^{p/2-1}\nabla f\right) + (p-2)\text{div}\left(f^{p/2-2}(\nabla u \otimes \nabla u)(\nabla f)\right) \\ &= f^{p/2-1}\Delta f + \left(\frac{p}{2}-1\right)f^{p/2-2}|\nabla f|^2 + (p-2)\text{div}\left(f^{p/2-2}(\nabla u \otimes \nabla u)(\nabla f)\right) \\ &= f^{p/2-1}\Delta f + \left(\frac{p}{2}-1\right)f^{p/2-2}|\nabla f|^2 \\ &\qquad+ (p-2)\left[f^{p/2-2}\text{div}\left((\nabla u \otimes \nabla u)(\nabla f)\right) + \left\langle \nabla\left(f^{p/2-2}\right),(\nabla u \otimes \nabla u)\nabla f\right\rangle\right] \\ &= f^{p/2-1}\Delta f + \left(\frac{p}{2}-1\right)f^{p/2-2}|\nabla f|^2 \\ &\qquad+ (p-2)\left[f^{p/2-2}\text{div}\left((\nabla u \otimes \nabla u)(\nabla f)\right) + \left(\frac{p}{2}-2\right)f^{p/2-3}\langle\nabla u, \nabla f\rangle^2\right] \\ &= f^{p/2-1}\Delta f + \left(\frac{p}{2}-1\right)f^{p/2-2}|\nabla f|^2 \\ &\qquad+ (p-2)\left[f^{p/2-2}\langle\nabla u,\nabla f\rangle\Delta u + f^{p/2-2}\left\langle\nabla(\langle\nabla u, \nabla f\rangle),\nabla u\right\rangle\right. \\ &\qquad+ \left.\left(\frac{p}{2}-2\right)f^{p/2-3}\langle\nabla u, \nabla f\rangle^2\right] \\ \end{aligned} \]

So we have the expression for \(\mathcal{L}(f)\)

\[ \begin{aligned} \mathcal{L}(f) &= f^{p/2-1}\Delta f + \left(\frac{p}{2}-1\right)f^{p/2-2}|\nabla f|^2 + (p-2)f^{p/2-2}\Delta u \langle \nabla u,\nabla f \rangle \\ &\qquad + (p-2)\left(\frac{p}{2}-2\right)f^{p/2-3}\langle\nabla u,\nabla f\rangle^2 + (p-2)f^{p/2-2}\left\langle\nabla(\langle\nabla u, \nabla f\rangle),\nabla u\right\rangle \\ &\qquad - pf^{p/2-1}\langle\nabla u,\nabla f\rangle. \end{aligned} \](3.5)

Taking the \(\nabla\) of (2.3) and then taking the inner product with \(\nabla u\), we have

\[ \begin{aligned} \frac{p}{2}f^{p/2-1}\langle\nabla f,\nabla u\rangle &= \left\langle\nabla\left(f^{p/2-1}\right),\nabla u\right\rangle\Delta u + f^{p/2-1}\langle\nabla\Delta u,\nabla u\rangle \\ &\qquad + \left\langle\nabla\left(\left(\frac{p}{2}-1\right)f^{p/2-2}\right),\nabla u\right\rangle\langle\nabla f,\nabla u\rangle + \left(\frac{p}{2}-1\right)f^{p/2-2}\langle\nabla(\langle\nabla f,\nabla u\rangle),\nabla\rangle \\ &= \left(\frac{p}{2}-1\right)f^{p/2-2}\langle\nabla f,\nabla u\rangle\Delta u + f^{p/2-1}\langle\nabla\Delta u,\nabla u\rangle \\ &\qquad + \left(\frac{p}{2}-1\right)\left(\frac{p}{2}-2\right)f^{p/2-3}\langle\nabla f,\nabla u\rangle^2 + \left(\frac{p}{2}-1\right)f^{p/2-2}\langle\nabla(\langle\nabla f,\nabla u\rangle),\nabla u\rangle. \end{aligned} \]

And two times the above coincides with the term we are subtracting in (3.5), so a lot of terms cancel out and we get a more simplified form of \(\mathcal{L}(f)\) as

\[ \mathcal{L}(f) = f^{p/2-1}\Delta f + \left(\frac{p}{2}-1\right)f^{p/2-2}|\nabla f|^2 - 2f^{p/2-1}\langle\nabla\Delta u,\nabla u\rangle. \]

Finally substituting the RHS of the Bochner formula (3.4) into the first term above and canceling, we arrive at

\[ \mathcal{L}(f) = 2f^{p/2-1}\left(u_{ij}^2+R_{ij}u_iu_j\right) + \left(\frac{p}{2}-1\right)f^{p/2-1}|\nabla f|^2 \]

as required.

4 A Parabolic Analog

In this section we consider the parabolic analog to the \(p\)-harmonic equation. Let \(u\) be a solution to the equation

\[ \frac{\partial u }{\partial t} - \Lambda_p(u) = \frac{\partial u}{\partial t} - \text{div}(|\nabla u|^{p-2}\nabla u) + |\nabla u|^p = 0. \](4.1)

The corresponding equation for \(v = \exp\left(-\frac{u}{p-1}\right)\) is

\[ \frac{\partial v^{p-1}}{\partial t} = (p-1)^{p-1}\text{div}(|\nabla v|^{p-2}\nabla v). \](4.2)

4.1 A Conservation Law

We now are moving toward a nonlinear entropy formula for this parabolic analog of the \(p\)-Laplacian. First, we need to establish the following proposition.

Proposition 4.3 ((Kotschwar and Ni 2009), Proposition 6.11). For \(v := \exp\left(-\frac{u}{p-1}\right)\) where \(u\) is a positive solution to (4.1) above (hence \(v\) is also positive), first observe that the quantity \(\int_M v^{p-1}d\mu\) is preserved under the equation (4.2). Then, for any smooth \(\psi\), we have the conservation law

\[ \frac{d}{dt}\int_M \psi v^{p-1}d\mu = \int_M\left(\left(\frac{\partial}{\partial t} - \mathcal{L}\right)\psi\right)v^{p-1}d\mu. \]

In particular, if \(\psi\) satisifies

\[ \left(\frac{\partial}{\partial t} - \mathcal{L}\right)\psi = 0, \](4.7)

then \(\int_M \psi v^{p-1}\) is constant along the equations (4.2) and (4.7).

Proof. By “the quantity \(\int_M v^{p-1}\) is preserved under the equation” we mean the following

\[ \frac{d}{dt}\int_M v^{p-1}d\mu = \int_M \frac{\partial v^{p-1}}{\partial t}d\mu = \int_M (p-1)^{p-1}\text{div}(|\nabla v|^{p-2}\nabla v)d\mu. \]

TODO: Prove this claim.

Now let \(\psi\) be smooth. It is easy enough to check that

\[ \nabla v = -\frac{v}{p-1}\nabla u, \](4.8)

and hence

\[\begin{aligned} (p-1)^{p-1}|\nabla v|^{p-2}\nabla v &= (p-1)^{p-1}\left|\frac{-v}{p-1}\nabla u\right|^{p-2}\left(-\frac{v}{p-1}\nabla u\right) \\ &= -v^{p-1}|\nabla u|^{p-2}\nabla u. \end{aligned}\](4.9)

Then we compute

\[\begin{aligned} \frac{d}{dt}\int_M \psi v^{p-1}d\mu &= \int_M \psi_t v^{p-1} + \psi \left(v^{p-1}\right)_t \\ &= \int_M \psi_t v^{p-1} - \mathcal{L}(\psi)v^{p-1} + \mathcal{L}(\psi)v^{p-1} + \psi\left(v^{p-1}\right)_t \\ &= \int_M \left(\left(\frac{\partial}{\partial t} - \mathcal{L}\right)\psi\right)v^{p-1} + \int_M \mathcal{L}(\psi)v^{p-1} + \int_M \psi \left(v^{p-1}\right)_t. \end{aligned}\](4.10)

By the definition of \(\mathcal{L}\) and integration by parts, the second integral above becomes

\[\begin{aligned} \int_M \mathcal{L}(\psi)v^{p-1} &= \int_M v^{p-1}\text{div}(|\nabla u|^{p-2}A(\nabla\psi)) - p\int_M |\nabla u|^{p-2}\langle\nabla u,\nabla\psi\rangle v^{p-1} \\ &= -\int_M \langle v^{p-1}, |\nabla u|^{p-2}A(\nabla \psi)\rangle d\mu + \int_{\partial M} v^{p-1}\langle |\nabla u|^{p-2}A(\nabla \psi), \vec{N}\rangle dS\mu \\ &\qquad- p\int_M |\nabla u|^{p-2}\langle \nabla u, \nabla \psi \rangle v^{p-1} \\ &= -\int_M |\nabla u|^{p-2}\langle\nabla A(\nabla\psi),\nabla v^{p-1}\rangle - p\int_M |\nabla u|^{p-2}\langle\nabla u,\nabla\psi\rangle v^{p-1}. \end{aligned}\](4.11)

where we are assuming \(\partial M = 0\).

Similarly, by (4.2) and (4.9), and then using integration by parts, the third integral in (4.10) becomes

\[\begin{aligned} \int_M \psi\left(v^{p-1}\right)_t &= \int_M \psi \text{div}\left((p-1)^{p-1}|\nabla u|^{p-2}\nabla u\right) \\ &= \int_M \psi \text{div}(-v^{p-1}|\nabla u|^{p-2}\nabla u) \\ &= -\int_M \langle \nabla\psi, -v^{p-1}|\nabla u|^{p-2}\nabla u\rangle d\mu + \int_{\partial M} \psi\langle -v^{p-1}|\nabla u|^{p-2}\nabla u,\vec{N}\rangle dS_\mu \\ &= \int_M |\nabla u|^{p-2}\langle \nabla\psi, \nabla u\rangle v^{p-1}. \end{aligned}\](4.12)

So combining (4.11) and (4.12) into (4.10), we arrive at

\[\begin{aligned} \frac{d}{dt}\int_M \psi v^{p-1} &= \int_M \left(\left(\frac{\partial}{\partial t} - \mathcal{L}\right)\psi\right)v^{p-1} -\int_M |\nabla u|^{p-2}\langle\nabla A(\nabla\psi),\nabla v^{p-1}\rangle \\ &\qquad -(p-1)\int_M |\nabla u|^{p-2}\langle \nabla\psi, \nabla u\rangle v^{p-1}. \end{aligned}\]

It remains to show that the last two integrals above cancel each other out. Observe that

\[ \nabla v^{p-1} = (p-1)v^{p-2}\nabla v = -v^{p-1}\nabla u. \]

Then expanding the definition for \(A\) and using the above, we have

\[\begin{aligned} -\int_M |\nabla u|^{p-2}\langle A(\nabla\psi),\nabla v^{p-1}\rangle &= -\int_M |\nabla u|^{p-2}\langle\nabla\psi,\nabla v^{p-1}\rangle \\ &\qquad - (p-2)\int_M |\nabla u|^{p-4}\langle\nabla\psi,\nabla u\rangle\langle\nabla u, \nabla v^{p-1}\rangle \\ &= (p-1)\int_M |\nabla u|^{p-2}v^{p-1}\langle \nabla \psi, \nabla u\rangle. \end{aligned}\]

And so the last two integrals indeed cancel out as required.

Remark 4.4. In the above, \(\psi\) doesn’t necessarily need to be smooth. The argument carries over assuming only the summability of the integrands.

4.2 The Entropy Formula

Let \((a_{ij})\) be the inverse of \((A^{ij})\), that is,

\[ a_{ij} = g_{ij} - \frac{p-2}{p-1}\frac{u_i u_j}{f}, \]

and can be viewed as a metric tensor. Let \(v\) be a positive solution to (4.2) normalized so that \(\int_M v^{p-1} = 1\). Let \(u := -\log(v^{p-1})\). Then we define the following quantities

\[\begin{aligned} &N(v,t) := \int_M v^{p-1} u d\mu \\ &\mathcal{N}_p(v,t) := N(v,t) - \frac{n}{p}\log{t} = \int_M v^{p-1} u d\mu - \frac{n}{p}\log{t} \\ &\mathcal{F}_p(v,t) := \frac{d}{dt}\mathcal{N}_p(v,t) \\ &\mathcal{W}_p(v,t) := \frac{d}{dt}\left(t\mathcal{N}_p(v,t)\right). \end{aligned} \]

We first establish the following proposition.

Proposition 4.5 ((Kotschwar and Ni 2009), Proposition 6.14). Recall that \(F_\alpha = |\nabla u|^p + \alpha u_t\) for any \(\alpha > 0\). In particular, \(F_1 = |\nabla u|^p + u_t\). Then

\[ \frac{dN}{dt} = \int_M |\nabla u|^p v^{p-1}d\mu = \int_M F_1 v^{p-1}d\mu = \int_M f^{p/2-1}\text{tr}_A(\nabla \nabla u)v^{p-1}d\mu. \](4.13)

Moreover, we have

\[ \frac{d}{dt}\int_M F_1 v^{p-1}d\mu = -p\int_M f^{p-2}(|\nabla\nabla u|_A^2 + R_{ij}u_iu_j)v^{p-1}d\mu. \](4.14)

Proof. We first prove (4.13). Note that the last equality above follows directly from (4.6), so it suffices to only show the first two equalities. By proposition 4.3, we have that

\[ \frac{dN}{dt} = \frac{d}{dt}\int_M v^{p-1} u d\mu = \int_M \left(\left(\frac{\partial}{\partial t} - \mathcal{L}\right)u\right)v^{p-1}d\mu. \](4.15)

So we compute \(\frac{\partial u}{\partial t} - \mathcal{L}(u)\):

\[\begin{aligned} \frac{\partial u}{\partial t} - \mathcal{L}(u) &= \Lambda_p(u) - \mathcal{L}(u) \\ &= \text{div}(|\nabla u|^{p-2}\nabla u) - |\nabla u|^p - \text{div}(|\nabla u|^{p-2}A(\nabla u)) + p|\nabla u|^{p-2}\langle \nabla u, \nabla u \rangle \\ &= \text{div}(|\nabla u|^{p-2}\nabla u) - \text{div}(|\nabla u|^{p-2}A(\nabla u)) + (p-1)|\nabla u|^{p} \\ &= \text{div}(|\nabla u|^{p-2}\nabla u) - \text{div}(|\nabla u|^{p-2}\nabla u) - (p-2)\text{div}(|\nabla u|^{p-2}\nabla u) + (p-1)|\nabla u|^p \\ &= (p-1)|\nabla u|^p - (p-2)\text{div}(|\nabla u|^{p-2}\nabla u). \end{aligned}\](4.16)

So substituting this into (4.15) above and continuing, we have

\[\begin{aligned} \frac{dN}{dt} &= \int_M \left(\left(\frac{\partial}{\partial t} - \mathcal{L}\right)u\right)v^{p-1}d\mu \\ &= (p-1)\int_M |\nabla u|^p v^{p-1} - (p-2)\int_M\text{div}(|\nabla u|^{p-2}\nabla u)v^{p-1} \\ &= (p-1)\int_M |\nabla u|^p v^{p-1} - (p-2)\left[-\int_M |\nabla u|^{p-2} \langle \nabla v^{p-1}, \nabla u \rangle d\mu \right. \\ &\qquad + \left.\int_{\partial M} |\nabla u|^{p-3}v^{p-1}\langle \nabla u, \vec{N}\rangle dS\mu\right] \\ &= (p-1)\int_M |\nabla u|^p v^{p-1} + (p-2)\int_M |\nabla u|^{p-2} \langle \nabla v^{p-1}, \nabla u \rangle \\ &= (p-1)\int_M |\nabla u|^p v^{p-1} - (p-2)\int_M |\nabla u|^{p-2} v^{p-1}\langle\nabla u,\nabla u\rangle \\ &= \int_M |\nabla u|^p v^{p-1} \end{aligned}\]

again using integration by parts and the fact that \(\nabla v^{p-1} = -v^{p-1}\nabla u\).

Now notice that

\[ F_1 = |\nabla u|^p + \frac{\partial u}{\partial t} = |\nabla u|^p + \text{div}(|\nabla u|^{p-2}\nabla u) - |\nabla u|^p = \text{div}(|\nabla u|^{p-2}\nabla u), \]

so we have that

\[ \int_M F_1 v^{p-1} = \int_M \text{div}(|\nabla u|^{p-2}\nabla u)v^{p-1} = -\int_m |\nabla u|^{p-2}\langle\nabla v^{p-1},\nabla u\rangle = \int_M |\nabla u|^p v^{p-1}, \]

which completes the proof of (4.13). The identity (4.14) follows directly from proposition 4.3 and the identity (4.5).

Let us define the “energy” by

\[ \overline{\mathcal{F}}(v,t) := \int_M |\nabla u|^p v^{p-1}d\mu = \int_M F_1 v^{p-1}d\mu. \]

Then since we are assuming \(v\) is a positive solution, if we further assume the Ricci curvature of \(M\) is nonnegative, then (4.14) is telling us that \(\frac{d}{dt}\overline{\mathcal{F}}(v,t) \leq 0\), i.e. that the energy is monotonically nonincreasing in \(t\).

Now let’s expand out the definitions of \(\mathcal{F}_p\) and \(\mathcal{W}_p\). For \(\mathcal{F}_p\), we have

\[ \mathcal{F}_p(v,t) = \frac{d}{dt}\mathcal{N}_p(v,t) = \frac{dN}{dt} - \frac{d}{dt}\left(\frac{n}{p}\log{t}\right) = \int_M|\nabla u|^p v^{p-1}d\mu - \frac{n}{pt}. \]

and for \(\mathcal{W}_p\), using the assumption that \(\int_M v^{p-1}d\mu = 1\), we have

\[\begin{aligned} \mathcal{W}_p(v,t) &= \frac{d}{dt}\left(t\mathcal{N}_p(v,t)\right) \\ &= \mathcal{N}_p(v,t) + t\frac{d}{dt}\mathcal{N}_p(v,t) \\ &= \int_M v^{p-1} u d\mu - \frac{n}{p}\log{t} + t\left(\int_M |\nabla u|^p v^{p-1}d\mu - \frac{n}{pt}\right) \\ &= \int_M (t|\nabla u|^p + u)v^{p-1}d\mu - \frac{n}{p}\log{t} - \frac{n}{p} \\ &= \int_M (t|\nabla u|^p + u)v^{p-1}d\mu - \int_M \left(\frac{n}{p}\log{t} + \frac{n}{p}\right)v^{p-1}d\mu \\ &= \int_M \left(t|\nabla u|^p + u - \frac{n}{p}\log{t} - \frac{n}{p}\right)v^{p-1}d\mu. \end{aligned}\](4.17)

Then, motivated by Equation (6.8) of (Kotschwar and Ni 2009), we can write \(v^{p-1}\) in the following form

\[ v^{p-1} := \frac{1}{\pi^{n/2}(p^{\ast p-1}p)^{n/p}}\frac{\Gamma(n/2+1)}{\Gamma(n/p^\ast + 1)}\frac{e^{-\varphi}}{t^{n/p}}, \]

where \(p^\ast := \frac{p}{p-1}\). For the sake of convenience, let’s also collect all the terms in the first two factors into a single constant \(G\), so we write \(v^{p-1} = G\frac{e^{-\varphi}}{t^{n/p}}\). Then since \(u = -\log{v^{p-1}}\), we have

\[ u = -\log{v^{p-1}} = -\log{G\frac{e^{-\varphi}}{t^{n/p}}} = -\log{G} + \varphi + \frac{n}{p}\log{t}. \](4.18)

So substituting this into our expanded expression for \(\mathcal{W}_p\) in (4.17), we have

\[\begin{aligned} \mathcal{W}_p(v,t) &= \int_M \left(t|\nabla u|^p + u - \frac{n}{p}\log{t} - \frac{n}{p}\right)v^{p-1}d\mu \\ &= \int_M \left(t|\nabla\left(-\log{G} + \varphi + \frac{n}{p}\log{t}\right)|^p - \log{G} + \varphi + \frac{n}{p}\log{t} - \frac{n}{p}\log{t} - \frac{n}{p}\right)v^{p-1}d\mu \\ &= \int_M \left(t|\nabla \varphi|^p - \log{G} + \varphi - \frac{n}{p}\right)v^{p-1}d\mu. \end{aligned}\]

There is a discrepancy here with the expression arrived at in (Kotschwar and Ni 2009) (middle of page 26), where they have

\[ \mathcal{W}_p(\varphi,t) = \int_M (t|\nabla \varphi|^p + \varphi - n)v^{p-1}d\mu. \](4.19)

Although it could be that \(-\log{G} - \frac{n}{p} = -n\), and a quick calculation shows that that would require \(p^\ast\log{G} = n\), but I haven’t checked the value of \(\log(G)\) yet. In any case, using the expression (4.19) and writing in terms of \(u\) and not \(\varphi\), since \(v^{p-1} = e^{-u}\), \(\varphi = u - \frac{n}{p}\log{t} + \log{G}\) and \(|\nabla u| = |\nabla \varphi|\), we have

\[ \mathcal{W}_p(u,t) = \int_M \left(t|\nabla u|^p + u - \frac{n}{p}\log{t} + \log{G} - n\right)e^{-u}d\mu, \]

which, when \(p = 1\) simplifies to

\[ \mathcal{W}_1(u,t) = \int_M\left(t|\nabla u| + u - n\log{t} + \log{\frac{\Gamma(n/2+1)}{\pi^{n/2}}} - n\right)e^{-u}d\mu. \]

When \(p = 2\), \(p^\ast = 2\), \(\Gamma(n/p^\ast + 1) = \Gamma(n/2+1)\) and \((p^{\ast p-1}p)^{n/p} = 4^{n/2}\), so we have

\[ \mathcal{W}_2(u,t) = \int_M\left(t|\nabla u|^2 + u - \frac{n}{2}\log{4\pi t} - n\right)e^{-u}d\mu, \]

which coincides with the entropy formula defined in Equation (1.2) of (Ni 2004).

The preceeding proposition allows us to establish the following nonlinear entropy formula

Theorem 4.6 (Theorem 6.13 of (Kotschwar and Ni 2009)). Let \(v\) be a positive solution to (4.2) satisfying \(\int_M v^{p-1}d\mu = 1\). Then

\[ \frac{d}{dt}\mathcal{W}_p(v,t) = -tp\int_M\left(\left|f^{p/2-1}\nabla_i\nabla_ju - \frac{1}{tp}a_{ij}\right|^2_A + f^{p-2}R_{ij}u_iu_j\right)v^{p-1}d\mu. \](4.20)

Note when \(p=2\), the entropy formula above coincides with the entropy formula arrived at in Theorem 1.1 of (Ni 2004).

Proof. In the paper, Kotschwar-Ni simply remark that the proof follows from proposition 4.5, observing that \(\frac{d\mathcal{W}_p}{dt} = t\frac{d\mathcal{F}_p}{dt} + 2\mathcal{F}_p\) and “completing the square”. So let’s do that.

We first verify that \(\frac{d\mathcal{W}_p}{dt} = t\frac{d\mathcal{F}_p}{dt} + 2\mathcal{F}_p\). We have

\[ \frac{d\mathcal{W}_p}{dt} = \frac{d}{dt}\left(\frac{d}{dt}(t\mathcal{N}_p)\right) = \frac{d\mathcal{N}_p}{dt} + \frac{d}{dt}\left(t\frac{d\mathcal{N}_p}{dt}\right) = \mathcal{F}_p + \frac{d}{dt}\left(t\mathcal{F}_p\right) = t\frac{d\mathcal{F}_p}{dt} + 2\mathcal{F}_p. \]

Then we use proposition 4.5 to fill in the definitions of \(2\mathcal{F}_p\) and \(t\frac{d\mathcal{F}_p}{dt}\). We have

\[\begin{aligned} 2\mathcal{F}_p &= 2\frac{d}{dt}\left(N - \frac{n}{p}\log{t}\right) \\ &= 2\frac{dN}{dt} - 2\frac{n}{pt} \\ &= \int_M \left(2f^{p/2-1}\text{tr}_A(\nabla \nabla A)\right)v^{p-1}d\mu - 2\frac{n}{pt}. \end{aligned}\]

And also

\[\begin{aligned} t\frac{d\mathcal{F}_p}{dt} &= t\frac{d}{dt}\left(\frac{dN}{dt} - \frac{n}{pt}\right) \\ &= t\frac{d}{dt}\int_M F_1v^{p-1}d\mu - t\frac{d}{dt}\frac{n}{pt} \\ &= t\frac{d}{dt}\int_M F_1v^{p-1}d\mu + \frac{n}{pt} \\ &= -tp\int_M f^{p-2}(|\nabla\nabla u|_A^2 + R_{ij}u_iu_j)v^{p-1}d\mu + \frac{n}{pt}. \end{aligned}\]

Then combining, we have

\[\begin{aligned} \frac{d\mathcal{W}_p}{dt} &= t\frac{d\mathcal{F}_p}{dt} + 2\mathcal{F}_p \\ &= -tp\int_M f^{p-2}(|\nabla\nabla u|_A^2 + R_{ij}u_iu_j)v^{p-1} + 2\int_M f^{p/2-1}\text{tr}_A(\nabla\nabla u)v^{p-1} - \frac{n}{pt} \\ &= \int_M \left[-tpf^{p-2}\left(|\nabla\nabla u|_A^2 + R_{ij}u_iu_j\right) + 2f^{p/2-1}\text{tr}_A(\nabla\nabla u) - \frac{n}{pt}\right]v^{p-1}d\mu. \end{aligned}\]

Now comes the “completing the square” step. Notice that \((f^{p/2-1})^2 = f^{p-2}\), so we can regard the \(-tpf^{p-2}|\nabla\nabla u|_A^2\) as the “square” term and \(2f^{p/2-1}\text{tr}_A(\nabla\nabla u)\) as the “linear” term. It is actually easier to “work backwards”. We have

\[\begin{aligned} \left|f^{p/2-1}\nabla_i\nabla_j u - \frac{1}{tp}a_{ij}\right|_A^2 &= \left\langle f^{p/2-1}\nabla_i\nabla_j u,\nabla_i\nabla_j u \right\rangle_A + 2\left\langle f^{p/2-1}\nabla_i\nabla_j u, -\frac{1}{tp}a_{ij}\right\rangle_A \\ &\qquad + \left\langle -\frac{1}{tp}a_{ij},-\frac{1}{tp}a_{ij}\right\rangle_A \\ &= \left(f^{p/2-1}\right)^2\left\langle\nabla_i\nabla_j u,\nabla_i\nabla_j u\right\rangle_A - \frac{2f^{p/2-1}}{tp}\left\langle \nabla_i\nabla_j u, a_{ij}\right\rangle_A \\ &\qquad + \frac{1}{t^2p^2}\left\langle a_{ij},a_{ij}\right\rangle_A \\ &= f^{p-2}|\nabla\nabla u|_A^2 - \frac{2f^{p/2-1}}{tp}\text{tr}_A(\nabla\nabla u) + \frac{n}{t^2p^2}. \end{aligned}\]

So multiplying both sides by \(-tp\), and incorporating, we finally arrive at

\[\begin{aligned} \frac{d\mathcal{W}_p}{dt} &= \int_M \left[-tpf^{p-2}\left(|\nabla\nabla u|_A^2 + R_{ij}u_iu_j\right) + 2f^{p/2-1}\text{tr}_A(\nabla\nabla u) - \frac{n}{pt}\right]v^{p-1}d\mu \\ &= -tp\int_M \left(\left| f^{p/2-1}\nabla_i\nabla_j u - \frac{1}{tp}a_{ij}\right|_A^2 + f^{p-2}R_{ij}u_iu_j\right)v^{p-1}d\mu, \end{aligned}\]

as required.

4.3 Monotonicity of the Entropy and the \(L^p\)-logarithmic Sobolev Inequality

Now we introduce the sharp \(L^p\)-logarithmic Sobolev inequality.

Theorem 4.7 (Theorem 6.17 of (Kotschwar and Ni 2009)). Let \(M\) be Euclidean space \(mathbb{R}^n\). Then for any \(p > 1\), and any \(w\in W^{1,p}(mathbb{R}^n)\) with \(\int |w|^pdx = 1\), we have

\[ \int |w|^p\log{|w|^p}dx \leq \frac{n}{p}\log{\left(\mathcal{C}_{p,n}\int|\nabla w|^p dx\right)} \](4.21)

where

\[ \mathcal{C}_{p,n} = \frac{p}{n}\left(\frac{p-1}{e}\right)^{p-1}\pi^{-p/2}\left(\frac{\Gamma(n/2+1)}{\Gamma(n/p^\ast + 1)}\right)^{p/n}. \]
