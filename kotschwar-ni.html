1 Introduction

These are some notes I’m writing while reading the Kotschwar-Ni paper (see (Kotschwar and Ni 2009)).

2 Initial Setup

Let \(p > 1\). A function \(v: M \to \mathbb{R}\) is called a \(p\)-harmonic function if it satisfies

\[ \text{div}\left(\left|\nabla v\right|^{p-2}\nabla v\right) = 0. \](2.1)

Note that when \(p = 2\), this reduces to the ordinary Laplace equation. Letting \(u := -(p-1)\log{v}\), then (2.1) becomes

\[ \text{div}\left(\left|\nabla u\right|^{p-2}\nabla u\right) - \left|\nabla u\right|^p = 0. \](2.2)

Note that when \(p = 1\), (2.2) above is the equation to the level-set formulation of the inverse mean curvature flow on a co-compact subdomain \(\Omega \subset \mathbb{R}^n\).

Let \(f := \left|\nabla u\right|^2\). Then the LHS of (2.2) becomes

\[ \begin{aligned} \text{div}\left(\left|\nabla u\right|^{p-2}\nabla u\right) &= \text{div}\left(f^{p/2-1}\nabla u\right) \\ &= f^{p/2-1}\text{div}\left(\nabla u\right) + \left\langle \nabla u, \nabla\left(f^{p/2-1}\right)\right\rangle \\ &= f^{p/2-1}\Delta u + \left(\frac{p}{2}-1\right)f^{p/2-2}\left\langle\nabla f, \nabla u\right\rangle. \end{aligned} \]

and the RHS of the same equation becomes \(\left|\nabla u\right|^{p} = \left(\left|\nabla u \right|^2\right)^{p/2} = f^{p/2}\). So we have that for \(f\), (2.2) is equivalent to

\[ f^{p/2-1}\Delta u + \left(\frac{p}{2}-1\right)f^{p/2-2}\langle\nabla f, \nabla u\rangle = f^{p/2}. \](2.3)

3 A Nonlinear Bochner-type Formula

Assume \(u, f > 0\) and \(u\) is a solution to (2.2) above. Let us denote by \(\Lambda_p\) the operator on the LHS of (2.2), that is,

\[ \Lambda_p(\varphi) := \text{div}\left(\left|\nabla \varphi\right|^{p-2}\nabla \varphi\right) - \left|\nabla \varphi\right|^p \]

and let \(L\) denote the linearization of \(\Lambda_p\) at \(u\).

We can compute what \(L\) must be by computing \(\frac{d}{d\varepsilon}\Big|_{\varepsilon=0}\Lambda_p(u+\varepsilon\varphi)\), i.e., taking the derivative at 0 of a small perturbation of \(\Lambda_p(u)\). Note that we have \(\nabla(u+\varepsilon\varphi) = \nabla u + \varepsilon\nabla\varphi\) and that

\[ \left|\nabla(u+\varepsilon\varphi)\right|^p = \left(\langle\nabla u + \varepsilon\nabla\varphi, \nabla u + \varepsilon\nabla\varphi\rangle\right)^{p/2} = \left(|\nabla u|^2 + 2\varepsilon\langle\nabla u,\nabla \varphi\rangle + \varepsilon^2|\nabla\varphi|^2\right)^{p/2} \]

So we have that

\[ \begin{aligned} \frac{d}{d\varepsilon}\Big|_{\varepsilon=0}|\nabla(u+\varepsilon\varphi)|^p &= \frac{d}{d\varepsilon}\Big|_{\varepsilon=0}\left(|\nabla u|^2 + 2\varepsilon\langle\nabla u,\nabla \varphi\rangle + \varepsilon^2|\nabla\varphi|^2\right)^{p/2} \\ &= \frac{p}{2}\left(|\nabla u|^2 + 2\varepsilon\langle\nabla u, \nabla \varphi\rangle + \varepsilon^2|\nabla\varphi|^2\right)^{p/2-1}\left(2\langle\nabla u, \nabla\varphi\rangle + 2\varepsilon|\nabla\varphi|^2\right)\Big|_{\varepsilon=0} \\ &= p\left(|\nabla u|^2\right)^{p/2-1}\langle\nabla u,\nabla\varphi\rangle \\ &= pf^{p/2-1}\langle\nabla u,\nabla\varphi\rangle. \end{aligned} \](3.1)

Similarly, we can evaluate \(\frac{d}{d\varepsilon}\Big|_{\varepsilon=0}\) of the expression inside the \(\text{div}\) operator. We have

\[ \begin{aligned} \frac{d}{d\varepsilon}\Big|_{\varepsilon=0}\left|\nabla(u+\varepsilon\varphi)\right|^{p-2}\nabla(u+\varepsilon\varphi) &= \frac{d}{d\varepsilon}\Big|_{\varepsilon=0}\left(|\nabla u|^2 + 2\varepsilon\langle\nabla u,\nabla \varphi\rangle + \varepsilon^2|\nabla\varphi|^2\right)^{p/2-1}\nabla(u+\varepsilon\varphi) \\ &\qquad + \left(|\nabla u|^2 + 2\varepsilon\langle\nabla u,\nabla \varphi\rangle + \varepsilon^2|\nabla\varphi|^2\right)^{p/2-1}\frac{d}{d\varepsilon}\Big|_{\varepsilon=0}(\nabla u + \varepsilon\nabla\varphi) \\ &= \left(\frac{p}{2}-1\right)\left(|\nabla u|^2\right)^{p/2-1}2\langle\nabla u, \nabla \varphi\rangle\nabla u + \left(|\nabla u|^2\right)^{p/2-1}\nabla\varphi \\ &= (p-2)f^{p/2-2}\langle\nabla u,\nabla\varphi\rangle\nabla u + f^{p/2-1}\nabla\varphi. \\ \end{aligned} \](3.2)

Notice that the tensor \(\nabla u \otimes \nabla u\) applied to the vector \(\nabla \varphi\) in coordinates is given by

\[ \begin{aligned} (\nabla u \otimes \nabla u)(\nabla\varphi) &= \left(u_i u_j dx^i\otimes dx^j\right)\left(g^{k\ell}\varphi_k\partial_\ell\right) \\ &= u_iu_jdx^i\left(g^{k\ell}\varphi_k\partial_\ell\right)dx^j \\ &= u_iu_jg^{k\ell}\varphi_k\delta^i_\ell dx^j \\ &= u_iu_j g^{ki}\varphi_kdx^j = \langle\nabla u,\nabla\varphi\rangle \nabla u. \end{aligned} \]

In view of this, we can define the tensor \(A:= \text{id} + (p-2)\frac{\nabla u \otimes \nabla u}{f}\) and rewrite the last line of (3.2) as

\[ \frac{d}{d\varepsilon}\Big|_{\varepsilon=0}\left|\nabla(u+\varepsilon\varphi)\right|^{p-2}\nabla(u+\varepsilon\varphi) = f^{p/2-1}A(\nabla\varphi). \]

So commuting \(\frac{d}{d\varepsilon}\) past the \(\text{div}\) operator, and combining the above with the last line of (3.1), all together we have

\[ L(\varphi) = \frac{d}{d\varepsilon}\Big|_{\varepsilon=0}\Lambda_p(u + \varepsilon\varphi) = \text{div}\left(f^{p/2-1}A(\nabla\varphi)\right) - pf^{p/2-1}\langle\nabla u,\nabla\varphi\rangle. \](3.3)

With this explicit expression for \(L\), Kotschwar and Ni then show the following lemma.

Lemma 3.1. [Kotschwar-Ni, Lemma 2.1] With \(u, f, A, L\) as above, we have the following expression for \(L(f)\).

\[ L(f) = 2f^{p/2-1}\left(u_{ij}^2 + R_{ij}u_iu_j\right) + \left(\frac{p}{2}-1\right)|\nabla f|^2f^{p/2-2}, \]

where \(u_{ij}\) is the Hessian of \(u\), \(R_{ij}\) is the Ricci curvature of \(M\).

Proof. The expression comes from computing \(L(f)\) using the expression we got in (3.3) above, and combining with the Bochner formula for \(f\).

Recall that the Bochner formula is given by the following.

\[ \Delta f = 2u_{ij}^2 + 2\langle\nabla\Delta u, \nabla u\rangle + 2R_{ij}u_iu_j. \](3.4)

By the definition of \(A\) and evaluating, we have

\[ \begin{aligned} \text{div}\left(f^{p/2-1}A(\nabla f)\right) &= \text{div}\left(f^{p/2-1}\left(\nabla f + (p-2)\frac{\nabla u \otimes \nabla u}{f}\nabla f\right)\right) \\ &= \text{div}\left(f^{p/2-1}\nabla f\right) + (p-2)\text{div}\left(f^{p/2-2}(\nabla u \otimes \nabla u)(\nabla f)\right) \\ &= f^{p/2-1}\Delta f + \left(\frac{p}{2}-1\right)f^{p/2-2}|\nabla f|^2 + (p-2)\text{div}\left(f^{p/2-2}(\nabla u \otimes \nabla u)(\nabla f)\right) \\ &= f^{p/2-1}\Delta f + \left(\frac{p}{2}-1\right)f^{p/2-2}|\nabla f|^2 \\ &\qquad+ (p-2)\left[f^{p/2-2}\text{div}\left((\nabla u \otimes \nabla u)(\nabla f)\right) + \left\langle \nabla\left(f^{p/2-2}\right),(\nabla u \otimes \nabla u)\nabla f\right\rangle\right] \\ &= f^{p/2-1}\Delta f + \left(\frac{p}{2}-1\right)f^{p/2-2}|\nabla f|^2 \\ &\qquad+ (p-2)\left[f^{p/2-2}\text{div}\left((\nabla u \otimes \nabla u)(\nabla f)\right) + \left(\frac{p}{2}-2\right)f^{p/2-3}\langle\nabla u, \nabla f\rangle^2\right] \\ &= f^{p/2-1}\Delta f + \left(\frac{p}{2}-1\right)f^{p/2-2}|\nabla f|^2 \\ &\qquad+ (p-2)\left[f^{p/2-2}\langle\nabla u,\nabla f\rangle\Delta u + f^{p/2-2}\left\langle\nabla(\langle\nabla u, \nabla f\rangle),\nabla u\right\rangle\right. \\ &\qquad+ \left.\left(\frac{p}{2}-2\right)f^{p/2-3}\langle\nabla u, \nabla f\rangle^2\right] \\ \end{aligned} \]

So we have the expression for \(L(f)\)

\[ \begin{aligned} L(f) &= f^{p/2-1}\Delta f + \left(\frac{p}{2}-1\right)f^{p/2-2}|\nabla f|^2 + (p-2)f^{p/2-2}\Delta u \langle \nabla u,\nabla f \rangle \\ &\qquad + (p-2)\left(\frac{p}{2}-2\right)f^{p/2-3}\langle\nabla u,\nabla f\rangle^2 + (p-2)f^{p/2-2}\left\langle\nabla(\langle\nabla u, \nabla f\rangle),\nabla u\right\rangle \\ &\qquad - pf^{p/2-1}\langle\nabla u,\nabla f\rangle. \end{aligned} \](3.5)

Taking the \(\nabla\) of (2.3) and then taking the inner product with \(\nabla u\), we have

\[ \begin{aligned} \frac{p}{2}f^{p/2-1}\langle\nabla f,\nabla u\rangle &= \left\langle\nabla\left(f^{p/2-1}\right),\nabla u\right\rangle\Delta u + f^{p/2-1}\langle\nabla\Delta u,\nabla u\rangle \\ &\qquad + \left\langle\nabla\left(\left(\frac{p}{2}-1\right)f^{p/2-2}\right),\nabla u\right\rangle\langle\nabla f,\nabla u\rangle + \left(\frac{p}{2}-1\right)f^{p/2-2}\langle\nabla(\langle\nabla f,\nabla u\rangle),\nabla\rangle \\ &= \left(\frac{p}{2}-1\right)f^{p/2-2}\langle\nabla f,\nabla u\rangle\Delta u + f^{p/2-1}\langle\nabla\Delta u,\nabla u\rangle \\ &\qquad + \left(\frac{p}{2}-1\right)\left(\frac{p}{2}-2\right)f^{p/2-3}\langle\nabla f,\nabla u\rangle^2 + \left(\frac{p}{2}-1\right)f^{p/2-2}\langle\nabla(\langle\nabla f,\nabla u\rangle),\nabla u\rangle. \end{aligned} \]

And two times the above coincides with the term we are subtracting in (3.5), so a lot of terms cancel out and we get a more simplified form of \(L(f)\) as

\[ L(f) = f^{p/2-1}\Delta f + \left(\frac{p}{2}-1\right)f^{p/2-2}|\nabla f|^2 - 2f^{p/2-1}\langle\nabla\Delta u,\nabla u\rangle. \]

Finally substituting the RHS of the Bochner formula (3.4) into the first term above and canceling, we arrive at

\[ L(f) = 2f^{p/2-1}\left(u_{ij}^2+R_{ij}u_iu_j\right) + \left(\frac{p}{2}-1\right)f^{p/2-1}|\nabla f|^2 \]

as required.
